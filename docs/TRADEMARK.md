# Trademark and Redistribution Policy

Helm's code is Apache-2.0. That license covers the code — it does not cover
the "AINumbers Helm" name, the Helm logo, or "Post Oak Labs" as a name. Those
are trademarks of Post Oak Labs, Inc., and this page explains what you can
and cannot do with them.

## You can

- Run Helm, modify it, and redistribute your modified version under
  Apache-2.0, same as any other Apache-2.0 project.
- Redistribute the verifier binary or the browser verifier unmodified,
  including as part of your own product or bundle.
- Say your product is "Powered by Helm" if it uses Helm's code, unmodified
  or modified.
- Link to the Helm repo, docs, or site when describing how your product
  uses Helm.

## You cannot

- Remove or hide the origin footer or attribution in the verifier when you
  redistribute it. The footer stays.
- Call your own fork or distribution "AINumbers Helm," "Helm," or anything
  that implies Post Oak Labs built or endorses it, if you didn't get that
  from us.
- Use the Helm name or logo in a way that implies your product is made by,
  certified by, or officially affiliated with Post Oak Labs, when it isn't.
- Register a domain, app name, or trademark that trades on "Helm" or
  "AINumbers" in a way likely to confuse people about who's behind it.

## Why this exists

Apache-2.0 grants you rights to the code. It does not grant you rights to
our name or logo (in fact, Apache-2.0 says exactly that, in section 6). This
page is the plain-language version of what that means in practice: fork
freely, redistribute the verifier, call yourself a Helm user — just don't
let anyone reading your materials think we made your fork or vouched for it.

## Questions

If you want to redistribute Helm under your own brand, offer white-label
distribution, or aren't sure whether something here covers your case, email
security@postoaklabs.com and we'll sort it out.
